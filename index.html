Visit Italy

Planning your first trip to Italy

Belinda Dixon Lonely Planet writer

Intro

Italy, located in Southern Europe, is known for its rich history, art, and culture. Home to iconic landmarks like the Colosseum and Leaning Tower of Pisa, Italy boasts a diverse landscape, including the Alps and Mediterranean coastline. Renowned for its cuisine, Italian food, featuring pasta and pizza, is celebrated worldwide. The capital, Rome, is a historical treasure trove, while cities like Florence and Venice showcase unique art and architecture. Italy has a parliamentary republic government and is a founding member of the European Union. With a vibrant lifestyle and contributions to art, fashion, and design, Italy remains a global cultural influencer.

Florence and Tuscany

art and wine

Italian art is a testament to centuries of creativity, seen in masterpieces like Michelangelo's David and Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa. From Renaissance to Baroque, Italy's artistic legacy is diverse and influential. Equally renowned is Italian wine, with regions like Tuscany and Piedmont producing world-class varieties like Chianti and Barolo. The intersection of art and wine reflects Italy's commitment to craftsmanship and cultural richness, inviting a sensory journey through its landscapes and traditions.
